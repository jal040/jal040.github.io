Hello again! I'm Jamie

I’m a designer with a background in human computer interaction and computer science. I've always loved to solve problems with unique solutions.

When I was young
I had always equated creativity with natural talent and I firmly believed I did not have that talent. I used to think that only certain gifted people could create beautiful things and so I steered clear of art-related activities. Math and science were the subjects I excelled at but they didn’t interest me career wise.
Entering undergraduate
I was excited at all the possibilities ahead of me. I dabbled in the thought of pre-med but I quickly realized I didn’t have the passion for medicine. One day I stumbled upon the term “user experience design” for the first time ever and I knew that this was what I was looking for. Even though I was skeptical about the “design” part at first, I decided to research how to become an artist. To my relief, I found that art is a skill more than anything else, which can be strengthened and practiced on! My mindset has changed from “I can’t because I’m not talented” to “I can because I will learn”.
Now
I’m thankful for the invaluable insights I’ve gained from being more naturally academic and building my artistic side. From my unique combination of education and experience, I have and continue to develop my problem solving skills. As an avid book worm, my empathy and perspective have only grown wider and deeper. As the designated family event planner, I find joy in putting together enjoyable experiences for everyone. As a designer, I explore possibilities and dissect problems to find the best solution.
